Project: Peptide Mark I - Autonomous Synthesis Platform

1. Project Overview
The Peptide Mark I is a high-purity, 3-layer anaerobic synthesizer designed to disrupt the laboratory hardware market. By replacing expensive, proprietary black-box systems with a transparent, gas-driven architecture, the Mark I achieves professional-grade results at approximately 2 percent of current market costs.

2. The Problem: The High-Cost Black Box
Current commercial peptide printers (CEM, Biotage, Gyros) cost between $50,000 and $150,000. These systems rely on:
- Expensive proprietary parts and service contracts.
- Mechanical pumps and valves prone to wear and cross-contamination.
- Closed-source software that prevents user-level optimization.

3. The Solution: Safe and Sane Engineering
The Mark I utilizes a stationary chemical library and a gas-pressure delivery system, eliminating the primary failure points of mechanical fluidics.

Key Technical Advantages:
- Stratified Argon Lake: Uses a 2-inch stagnant layer of Argon gas to provide superior anaerobic protection for the reaction vessel.
- Argon Piston Fluidics: Moves reagents via regulated gas pressure instead of mechanical pumps, ensuring zero wear in the chemical path.
- Boss-Worker Architecture: Employs a Python-based control layer (Boss) and an Arduino-based execution layer (Worker) for transparent, reliable operation.
- Small Footprint: A compact 600mm square design suitable for desktop lab environments.

4. Financial Impact
- Current Industry Entry Cost: $50,000.00+
- Peptide Mark I Build Cost: ~$1,500.00
- Maintenance Cost: User-serviceable with off-the-shelf components.

5. Market Positioning
The Peptide Mark I is positioned for research institutions, boutique biotech startups, and independent developers who require high-purity synthesis without the prohibitive overhead of Tier 1 commercial hardware. It is built on a philosophy of safe, environmentally sane, and accessible engineering.
