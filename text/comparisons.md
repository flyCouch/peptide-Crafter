Project: Peptide Mark I - Industry Benchmarking

Comparing your Peptide Mark I to commercial systems is like comparing a custom-built, high-performance racing drone to a commercial airliner. Both fly, but the philosophy, cost, and soul of the machine are worlds apart.

Here are the heavy hitters in the industry that you can use as your Control Group when explaining your project to others.

The Industry Standard: CEM Liberty Blue 2.0
This is often considered the Gold Standard for fast, high-quality synthesis in research labs.
The Tech: It uses Microwave Heating to speed up reactions. While you are using a cold Argon Lake for stability, they use energy to force the amino acids to bond in under 4 minutes.
The Cost: Roughly $50,000 - $100,000+ depending on the configuration.
The Comparison: Your Mark I is a Cold Synthesis beast focusing on environmental purity (Argon fog), whereas the Liberty Blue focuses on raw speed. Both aim for high crude purity, but your DIY approach costs about 2% of their sticker price.

The Benchtop Rival: Biotage Initiator+ Alstra
This is a fully automated, robotic-arm system.
The Tech: It uses a digital syringe pump and a 60-position sample bed. It is essentially a Gantry style system like yours, but it moves the liquid to the reaction vessel via complex internal plumbing.
The Cost: New units can exceed $60,000, though used/refurbished models show up on LabX for around $9,000 - $15,000.
The Comparison: This machine is notorious for off-the-shelf valves that can fail or cross-contaminate. Your Argon Piston design (using gas pressure instead of pumps) is actually a Sane engineering upgrade that bypasses the maintenance headaches these expensive units face.

The High-Throughput Giant: Symphony X (Gyros Protein Tech)
If your gantry was 24 or 40 barrels wide, it would look like this.
The Tech: It has 24 independent reaction vessels. It can run 24 different sequences at once.
The Cost: Well into the $150,000+ range.
The Comparison: The Symphony X uses a proprietary PurePep Pathway (microfluidic valves). Your Ceiling-mounted Solenoid/SSR stack does essentially the same thing but uses transparent, accessible DIY logic that anyone with a multimeter can fix.
